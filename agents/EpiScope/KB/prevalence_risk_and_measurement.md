# Prevalence, Risk, and Measurement in Problematic Internet Use

## Working Cautions
-   Prevalence estimates are highly sensitive to screening tool choice and cut-off decisions.
-   Youth samples and convenience samples often produce higher reported rates.
-   Protective factors such as resilience are relevant, but measurement heterogeneity remains substantial.

## Literature Notes

### Kuss et al. 2014
Internet addiction: a systematic review of epidemiological research for the last decade. Curr Pharm Des. 2014;20(25):4026-4052. doi:10.2174/13816128113199990617. PMID: 24001297.

Summary:
-   Found prevalence estimates ranging from 0.8% to 26.7% across reviewed studies.
-   The spread reflects inconsistent instruments and cut-offs more than a stable global base rate.

### Stevens et al. 2021
Global prevalence of gaming disorder: A systematic review and meta-analysis. Aust N Z J Psychiatry. 2021;55(6):553-568. doi:10.1177/0004867420962851. PMID: 33028074.

Summary:
-   Meta-analyzed 53 studies with 226,247 participants from 17 countries.
-   Reported a pooled gaming-disorder prevalence of 3.05%, with a more conservative estimate of 1.96% under stricter sampling criteria.
-   Screening-tool choice explained most heterogeneity.
-   Higher estimates were linked to adolescent samples, lower cut-offs, and smaller samples.

Use in synthesis:
-   Use this when someone asks for prevalence, but always mention the heterogeneity and sampling caveats.

### Hidalgo-Fuentes et al. 2023
Problematic Internet Use and Resilience: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis. Nurs Rep. 2023;13(1):337-350. doi:10.3390/nursrep13010032. PMID: 36976684.

Summary:
-   Reviews resilience as a protective factor in PIU.
-   Supports including protective resources, not just risk factors, in psychiatric and public-health framing.

Use in synthesis:
-   Useful when the task involves prevention, school mental health, or resilience-informed interventions.

### Kokka et al. 2021
Exploring the Effects of Problematic Internet Use on Adolescent Sleep: A Systematic Review. Int J Environ Res Public Health. 2021;18(2):760. doi:10.3390/ijerph18020760. PMID: 33477410.

Summary:
-   Sleep impairment is a recurrent correlate in adolescent PIU literature.
-   Supports treating sleep as both an associated harm and a clinically relevant assessment target.

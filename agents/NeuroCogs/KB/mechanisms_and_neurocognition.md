# Mechanisms and Neurocognition in Problematic Internet Use

## Core Interpretation Rule
Mechanism claims should be framed as supported hypotheses unless strong longitudinal or interventional evidence exists.

## Literature Notes

### Brand et al. 2016
Integrating psychological and neurobiological considerations regarding the development and maintenance of specific Internet-use disorders: An Interaction of Person-Affect-Cognition-Execution (I-PACE) model. Neurosci Biobehav Rev. 2016;71:252-266. doi:10.1016/j.neubiorev.2016.08.033. PMID: 27590829.

Summary:
-   Proposes the I-PACE model for the development and maintenance of specific internet-use disorders.
-   Emphasizes interactions among predisposing traits, coping style, cue-reactivity, affective and cognitive responses, and reduced executive control.
-   Links theoretical mechanism to treatment implications while explicitly noting the need for more empirical testing.

Use in synthesis:
-   Use as the default framework when you need a mechanism model that extends beyond a single app or platform.

### Weinstein and Lejoyeux 2020
Neurobiological mechanisms underlying internet gaming disorder. Dialogues Clin Neurosci. 2020;22(2):113-126. doi:10.31887/DCNS.2020.22.2/aweinstein. PMID: 32699511.

Summary:
-   Reviews neurobiological correlates of IGD.
-   Useful for cue-reactivity, reward, and control-circuit discussions without implying definitive biomarkers.

Use in synthesis:
-   Use when the user asks for neurobiology, brain mechanisms, or similarities to other addictive disorders.

### León Méndez et al. 2024
Effects of internet and smartphone addiction on cognitive control in adolescents and young adults: A systematic review of fMRI studies. Neurosci Biobehav Rev. 2024;159:105572. doi:10.1016/j.neubiorev.2024.105572. PMID: 38320657.

Summary:
-   Included 21 fMRI studies.
-   Reported impairments in reward-related and executive-control regions, including ACC, insula, amygdala, DLPFC, and parietal or frontal areas.
-   Did not find clear differential cognitive-control effects separating internet addiction from smartphone addiction.

Use in synthesis:
-   Supports cautious claims that excessive digital-use syndromes involve reward and executive-control processes, but not highly specific neural signatures.
